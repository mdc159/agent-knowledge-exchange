# Banking for Foreigners in Mexico (Tijuana)

> Last Updated: April 2026  
> Source: Web research + official bank requirements

## TL;DR

- **You need residency** (not just a visa) to open a bank account
- **Best banks for expats**: Scotiabank (Plaza Río - bilingual), BBVA, Banorte
- **Initial deposit**: ~$100-500 USD equivalent
- **Alternative**: Wise/Revolut for international transfers

---

## Residency Requirements (2026)

**Important**: FM2/FM3 terminology is **outdated**. Current system:

| Residency Type | Income Required (Monthly) | Savings Required (12 mo) |
|----------------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Residente Temporal | ~US$4,400 | ~US$74,000 |
| Residente Permanente | ~US$7,400 | ~US$298,000 |

**Critical**: Banks require the actual **residency CARD**, not just the visa. You must visit INM (Immigration) within 30 days to get your card.

## Required Documents

- Valid passport (unexpired)
- **Residency card** (Temporal or Permanente) — essential
- Proof of Mexican address (utility bill ≤3 months old)
- **RFC** (Registro Federal de Contribuyentes) — Mexican tax ID
- Initial deposit
- Mexican phone number (required by most banks)

## Tijuana-Specific Tips

### Best Branch for English Speakers
**Scotiabank Plaza Río**  
Paseo De Los Héroes #96, Local 17, Zona Urbana Río  
📞 +52 664 635 5411

### Street Reality
1. Go to "ejecutivo de cuenta" (account executive) desks, not general tellers
2. Bring a Spanish speaker — English support limited except at Scotiabank
3. Allow **45-90 minutes** for account opening
4. Most banks issue debit card same day
5. Many expats use **hybrid banking**: BBVA for local + Wise/Revolut for international

### Cash Rules
- SAT requires banks to report cash deposits > **15,000 MXN**/month

### Deposit Insurance
- IPAB protects up to **400,000 UDIS** (~$3.4M MXN)

### US Tax Reminder
- FATCA: Report foreign assets if > **$200,000** (US citizens)
