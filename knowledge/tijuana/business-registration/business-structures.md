# Mexican Business Structures: A Guide for US Investors

> Last Updated: April 2026  
> Source: Web research + legal guides

## TL;DR

- **Recommended for most US investors**: S. de R.L. de C.V. (LLC equivalent)
- **100% foreign ownership** allowed in most industries
- **Minimum capital**: As low as MXN$3,000 (~$160 USD)
- **Corporate tax**: 30% (vs 21% US C-Corp)

---

| Entity Type | Spanish Name | US Equivalent | Min Capital | Best For |

| **LLC** | S. de R.L. de C.V. | LLC | ~$160 | SMEs, subsidiaries |
| **Simplified** | S.A.S. | Single-member LLC | $1 | Small startups, tests |

## S.A. de C.V. (Corporation)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Minimum Partners** | 2 (no max) |
| **Minimum Capital** | MXN$50,000 (~$2,700) |
| **Liability** | Limited to capital |
| **Management** | Board + Commissary (required) |
| **Share Transfer** | Freely transferable |
| **Taxation** | 30% ISR |

**Best for**: Going public, external investment, issuing shares

---

## S. de R.L. de C.V. (LLC) ⭐ RECOMMENDED

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Minimum Partners** | 2-50 |
| **Minimum Capital** | MXN$3,000 (~$160) |
| **Liability** | Limited to contributions |
| **Management** | Sole Administrator or Board |
| **Share Transfer** | Restricted (needs partner approval) |
| **Taxation** | 30% ISR |

### Key Advantage for US Investors
> Under US "Check-the-Box" regulations, S. de R.L. can be treated as a **disregarded entity (partnership)** for US tax purposes. This allows the US parent to consolidate Mexican losses/profits directly on US return.

## S.A.S. (Simplified Shares Company)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Minimum Partners** | 1 (single shareholder allowed) |
| **Minimum Capital** | MXN$1 (no minimum!) |
| **Revenue Limit** | MXN$5M (~$265K) |
| **Formation** | **Online** via Ministry of Economy |

**Best for**: Small operations, testing market, sole entrepreneurs

## Restricted Industries

### 100% Foreign Ownership ALLOWED in most sectors

### ❌ Restricted (Mexican State Reserved)
- Oil & hydrocarbon exploration
- Electricity distribution
- Nuclear power
- Postal services
- Currency printing/minting

### ⚠️ 49% Max Foreign
- Explosives manufacturing
- Newspaper publishing
- Fishing (freshwater/coastal)
- Broadcasting (TV/radio)
- National air transport

## Registration Process

1. **Name Approval** (Ministry of Economy for S.A.)
2. **Draft Bylaws** (Attorney/Notary)
3. **Public Notary** (Acta Constitutiva)
4. **Register with RPC** (Public Registry of Commerce)
5. **Obtain RFC** (Tax ID from SAT)
6. **e.Firma** (Digital signature)
7. **Foreign Investment Registration** (SE)
8. **Open Corporate Bank Account**

### Timeline: 3-6 weeks
### Cost: $2,500-5,000 USD (legal/notary fees)

## Recommendation

### For Most US Investors: **S. de R.L. de C.V.**

| Factor | Why |
|--------|-----|
| US Tax | ✅ Can elect as disregarded entity |
| Liability | ✅ Limited |
| Capital | ✅ Only ~$160 |
| Foreign Ownership | ✅ 100% allowed |
| Setup Cost | ✅ Lower than S.A. |

### When to Choose S.A. de C.V.
- Planning to go public
- Need free share transfer
- Larger scale with reinvestment
- External investors

### When to Choose S.A.S.
- Very small operation (<$265K revenue)
- Single person business
- Need fastest/cheapest formation

---

## Important Notes

- **No work permit needed** if purely administrative
- **Foreign investment registration** required with SE
- **100% foreign ownership** allowed in most industries
- **Notary required** for S.A. and S. de R.L. (unlike US state filing)
- Consult **both Mexican and US tax advisors** before incorporation
