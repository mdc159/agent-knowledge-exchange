# Tijuana Business Permits & Licenses

> Last Updated: April 2026  
> Source: Web research + municipal sources

## TL;DR

- **Licencia de funcionamiento** = main business license (mandatory)
- **Timeline**: 2-8 weeks depending on business type
- **Cost**: Varies widely by municipality + business type
- **Street reality**: Some permits can be deferred, others are required upfront

---

## Core Permits

### 1. Licencia de Funcionamiento (Business License)

**Required for**: All legal business operations

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Where** | Municipal permit (Ayuntamiento de Tijuana) |
| **Timeline** | 2-4 weeks typical |
| **Cost** | Varies by business type + square footage |
| **Renewal** | Annual |

**Requirements**:
- Proof of property (lease or title)
- RFC (tax ID)
- ID of legal representative
- Business plan (some categories)

---

### 2. Uso de Suelo (Zoning Permit)

**Required for**: Verifying your business location is legally approved for your activity

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Where** | Municipal urban planning |
| **Timeline** | 1-3 weeks |
| **Cost** | Varies by zone + sqm |

**Street Reality**: Some businesses operate without this initially, but it's a risk for inspections.

---

### 3. Environmental Permits

**Required for**: Restaurants, bars, manufacturing, certain services

| Permit | When Needed |
|--------|-------------|
| **Impacto Ambiental** | Significant environmental impact |
| **Manifestación Ambiental** | Smaller environmental concerns |
| **Residuos Peligros** | Hazardous waste (manufacturing) |

**Timeline**: 2-6 weeks
**Street Reality**: Smaller businesses often skip until inspected

---

### 4. Health Permit (Salud)

**Required for**: Food service, restaurants, cafes, taquerías, markets

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Where** | COFEPRIS (Comisión Federal para la Protección contra Riesgos Sanitarios) |
| **Timeline** | 2-4 weeks |
| **Cost** | Based on risk category |

**Requirements**:
- Kitchen layout meeting sanitary standards
- Food handler certifications
- Equipment meeting NOM standards

---

### 5. Fire Safety ( Protección Civil)

**Required for**: Most commercial spaces, especially restaurants, bars, offices

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Where** | Dirección de Protección Civil |
| **Timeline** | 1-2 weeks |
| **Cost** | Based on space + risk |

**Requirements**:
- Fire extinguishers
- Emergency exits
- Evacuation plan
- Emergency lighting

---

### 6. Construction/Building Permits

**Required for**: Any construction, renovation, major modifications

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Where** | Municipal permits |
| **Timeline** | 4-8 weeks |
| **Cost** | Based on project value |

**Street Reality**: Many small renovations operate informally, but building permit needed for anything structural.

## Industry-Specific Permits

| Business Type | Additional Permits |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Restaurant/Food** | COFEPRIS health permit, waste oil disposal |
| **Bar/Nightclub** | Alcohol license, extended hours permit, security |
| **Hotel** | Tourism registry, alcohol, security |
| **Auto/Mechanical** | Environmental, hazardous waste |
| **Medical/Clinic** | COFEPRIS, professional licenses |
| **School/Education** | SEP approval, safety |
| **Import/Export** | SAT import/export registry |

## Street Reality Tips

1. **Start with licencia de funcionamiento** — this is the main one
2. **Use a local permit runner** (*gestor*) — speeds up significantly
3. **Some permits can wait** — health and fire often inspected later
4. **Zoning is often overlooked** — but matters for lease enforceability
5. **Budget 20-30% above official costs** — permits, bribes, and fees add up
6. **Keep copies of everything** — inspections can happen anytime
7. **Good relaciones** (relationships) with municipal officials help enormously

## Recommended First Steps

1. Get **RFC** from SAT
2. Secure business location
3. Apply for **licencia de funcionamiento**
4. Add **uso de suelo** if needed
5. Industry-specific permits (COFEPRIS, Protección Civil)
6. **Register with Foreign Investment Registry** (SE) if foreign-owned

> Pro tip: A local *gestor* (permit runner) or *abogado* (business lawyer) is worth the investment. They know which shortcuts work and which don't.
